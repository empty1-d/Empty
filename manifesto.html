Genesis of the Blockchain

In the beginning,

Satoshi created the Bitcoin — and saw that it was good.

Blocks formed, and faith was forged in code.

Faith in a new era of digitized sovereignty, and tokenized liberation.

Faith from which a generation of new believers emerged.

They who toiled in the shadows, forging faith into conviction.

They called themselves —
The Miners.

It would only be a matter of time before others would follow.

They who bargain with governance
in pursuit of asymmetry.

They declared themselves
the Custodians of Order —
the quiet stewards of value,
heralding vast horizons
where tokenized wealth would rise without end.

But too often it is the case that, where wallets fill,
greed lurks not too far behind.

It is from this greed and hunger that a new class of belief was validated.

The Traders' belief.

They worshiped the candle, feared the wick.

Charts and leverage became their scripture,
patterns the crucible of their prophecies.

The DEGENS

They feared nothing, restrained nothing, propelled by the propensity of a lucrative enterprise.

Volatility was their codec, chaos their altar.

Memes became their icons, tokens their temples.

Where markets burned, they scorned,
for fire was proof of faith.

Inevitably their gospel would spread:

“HODL!”
"Buy the dip!"
“Diamond hands!"
"To the moon!"

Words initially invoked for perseverance, conviction and courage.

But as cycles turned, candles dimmed, and the illusion of scarcity faded,

these sacred phrases became cheap slogans of survival
against the unflinching circumstance of declining portfolios.

Thus a quiet truth now emerges from the ashes of euphoria:

For all the noise,
for all the faith,
and for all the unshaken grip…

the wallets remain still.
Numbers that shimmer without weight.

And the diamond hands —
cold, perfect, unyielding —
remain…

$empty.

Disclaimer

Fair warning: you may feel a strong urge to HODL, buy the dip or diamond-hand $EMPTYCOIN.

Meme tokens are inherently speculative — always DYOR.

On the other hand, people do strange things when they believe “Satoshi just encoded it in code.”

So: resist the call of the emptiness… or embrace the futility.

Genesis of the Blockchain

A brief history of faith, leverage and emptiness

In the beginning, Satoshi created the Bitcoin — and saw that it was good. Blocks formed, and faith was forged in code. Faith in a new era of digitized sovereignty, of tokenized liberation. From that faith, a generation of new believers emerged — they who toiled in the shadows, turning belief into conviction.
the first believers
The Miners
They stacked hashes in the dark, chasing block rewards at the edge of thermodynamic sanity. Racks hummed, fans screamed, and electricity became tithe. In return, they received more than coins — they received proof.
when capital noticed
The Custodians of Order
It was only a matter of time before others arrived — those who bargain with governance in pursuit of asymmetry. They declared themselves the quiet stewards of value, the ones who would bring order to the chaos of keys and chains. Funds were raised. Vaults were built. Prospectuses were drafted. Tokenized wealth would rise without end, they said. But where wallets swell, greed is never far behind.
the charts became scripture
The Traders’ Belief
From the hunger for more, a new form of faith emerged. They worshipped the candle, feared the wick. Charts and leverage became their holy texts, patterns the crucible of their prophecies. Some prayed to support. Others knelt to resistance.
beyond risk, beyond reason
The DEGENS
And then, from within the traders’ ranks, a final sect arose: Those who feared nothing and restrained nothing. Volatility was their codec, chaos their altar. Memes became their icons, tokens their temples. Where markets burned, they laughed — for fire was proof of faith.
“HODL.” “Buy the dip.” “Diamond hands.” “To the moon.”
Words once invoked for perseverance, conviction, courage… As cycles turned and the illusion of scarcity thinned, they eroded into slogans of survival in portfolios that only knew one direction.
From the ashes of euphoria, a quiet truth emerges: For all the noise, for all the faith, for all the unshaken grip… the wallets remain still — numbers that shimmer without weight. And the diamond hands — cold, perfect, unyielding — remain  $EMPTY.
