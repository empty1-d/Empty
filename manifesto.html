The Empty Manifesto

In the beginning, Satoshi created the Bitcoin — and saw that it was good.

Blocks formed, and faith was forged in code.

Faith in a new era of digitized sovereignty and tokenized liberation. Faith from which a generation of new believers emerged — they who toiled in the shadows, forging faith into conviction.

The Miners

They called themselves the Miners. It would only be a matter of time before others would follow.

The Custodians

Then came those who bargain with governance in pursuit of asymmetry. They declared themselves the Custodians of Order — quiet stewards of value, heralding vast horizons where tokenized wealth would rise without end.

But where wallets fill, greed rarely lags behind. From that hunger, a new class of belief was validated.

The Traders

The Traders worshiped the candle and feared the wick. Charts and leverage became their scripture; patterns, the crucible of their prophecies.

The Degens

From within their ranks, another sect emerged — the DEGENS.

They feared nothing, restrained nothing, propelled by the possibility of a lucrative exit. Volatility was their codec; chaos, their altar. Memes became their icons; tokens, their temples. Where markets burned, they laughed — for fire was proof of faith.

Inevitably, their gospel spread: “HODL.” “Buy the dip.” “Diamond hands.” “To the moon.”

Words once invoked for perseverance, conviction, and courage. But as cycles turned, candles dimmed, and the illusion of scarcity thinned, these phrases decayed into slogans of survival — chanted against the unflinching arithmetic of declining portfolios.

Thus a quiet truth emerges from the ashes of euphoria: for all the noise, for all the faith, for all the unshaken grip…

the wallets remain still — numbers that shimmer without weight.

And the diamond hands — cold, perfect, unyielding — remain… $empty.

Fair warning. You may feel a strong urge to HODL, buy the dip, or even diamond hand $EMPTYCOIN.

Our take: meme tokens are inherently speculative. Always DYOR.

On the other hand, people do strange things when they believe “Satoshi just encoded fate in code.”

So: resist the call of the emptiness… or embrace the futility.
